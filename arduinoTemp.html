Arduino Thermometer With LCD

Temperature Formula

The thermistor changes its resistance due to a change in temperature. By measuring the voltage from the thermistor at room temperature and at 59 degrees, a slope and equation can be made. Although the relation between resistance and temperature is not quite linear, a linear approximation can be used to convert the voltage reading and temperature.

LCD Monitor

Adding an LCD to this project helped bring a connection between the user and the project. By adding a potentiometer to the third pin of the LCD, the brightness could be controlled by increasing or decreasing the voltage.

Goals and Proceedure

The goal of this project was to create a device that could detect and display the change in temperature of the atmosphere. The original design was meant mainly for indoor use and for temperatures as close to room temperature as possible. The further the temperature gets from 68 degrees Fahrenheit, the less accurate it will be. Using both Arduino circuiting skills and thermodynamic skills, the device that acts as a very sensitive and accurate thermometer.

The whole device counts on the unique ability of the thermistor. Because resistance in circuits can fluctuate due to the change in atmosphere, a mathematical pattern can be determined. The temperature reading circuit consists of the thermistor and a simple resistor. Using Ohm's Law for resistance in series, one pin of the Arduino can read the voltage due to the thermistor. From there, a linear equation can turn that voltage into a temperature reading.

As with many hardware projects, my main problem came with wiring. My breadboard is not the best and the 5V wire for both the LCD display and thermistor circuit came loose. This can be especially difficult when dealing with an LCD that has 16 pins that all need to be connected to 5V, ground, or the Arduino. If I were to do the project again, I would get a better breadboard.
